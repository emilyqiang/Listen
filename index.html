GENDER BIAS IN HEALTHCARE

Gender bias in healthcare is a systemic issue that negatively impacts the quality of care that women, transgender and non-binary individuals receive.
Gender stereotypes that healthcare practitioners hold often lead to misdiagnoses, delayed treatments and inadequate care that can be ultimately fatal.

"Listen" is a role-playing game designed to empower young women with the skills needed to navigate and address gender biases in healthcare.

Through interactive gameplay, players learn essential skills in research, preparation, and self-advocacy to help protect themselves from healthcare disparities.

Gameplay


Conversational Battles


Players must choose the right dialogue options to earn persuasion points. The amount of persuasion points earned by the player before their health runs out determines their ending. Choose wisely as the consequences of selecting the wrong option could lead to your demise.




Character Perks


Each character has their unique perk that makes the difficulty of the game harder by giving additional bonuses or penalties to persuasion points.




Room Exploration


Find various items around your room to reveal information crucial to guiding the selection of correct dialogue choices




Medicine Cabinet


Fill up your medicine cabinet with trophies of empty pill bottles from successful doctor's appointments!

User-Research


Interviews



Interviews were conducted with 4 women in their 20's. The insight gathered from this research developed my key persona and her needs.
